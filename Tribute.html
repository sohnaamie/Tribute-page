Cheikh Anta Diop (1923-1986)

The man who revolutionized our understanding of African history.

Distinguished historian and Pan-Africanist political leader, Cheikh Anta Diop was born in Diourbel, Senegal on December 23, 1933 to a Muslim Wolof family. He argued that the origins of civilization and human development trace back to Africa, particularly ancient Egypt. He challenged the widespread belief that African cultures were inferior by showcasing how African people were the true pioneers of science, mathematics, and culture. Diop’s work emphasized that the foundation of human progress and civilization started in Africa, and he sought to correct historical misconceptions by highlighting Africa’s central role in shaping the world.

Cheikh Anta Diop aimed to promote the idea that African identity, culture, and history should be reclaimed and celebrated, while encouraging African unity and self-determination. Diop's work spanned across multiple disciplines, including history, linguistics, archaeology, and genetics, all in an effort to reassert the significance of Africa in the global historical narrative.

As a renowned scholar and Political activist Diop was appointed professor of ancient history at Dakar University in 1980. Over his career Diop published a number of books including seven which were translated into English. His most famous works were The african-origin-civilization-myth-or-reality (1974) The cultural-unity-black-africa, and Towards the African Renaissance: Essays in African Culture and Development, 1946-1960 (1978).
